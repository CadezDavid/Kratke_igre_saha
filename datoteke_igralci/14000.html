ZBYNEK HRACEK
(born Sep-09-1970, 50 years old) Czech Republic
[what is this?]

Grandmaster, Czech champion in 1994. He qualified for the World Cup (2013) from a strong result in the European Individual Championships (2013). At the World Cup, he lost to Moldavan veteran GM Viktor Bologan in the first round and was eliminated from the contest. Playing board 5 for his team G-Team Novy Bor, he helped it to win the European Club Cup (2013).

In 1995 (July - December Elo list), Hracek was ranked as no. 24-28 worldwide, alongside with Viktor Korchnoi, Peter Svidler, Judit Polgar, and Curt Hansen (ordered by number of games).

In 1996 (January - June Elo list), Hracek was in the Top Twenty of the world as shared no. 19-21, and again the leading Czech player.

He played for Czechoslovakia at the Chess Olympiads of 1990 and 1992, and for the Czech Republic at the Olympiads of 1994, 1996, 2000, 2002, 2004, 2006, 2008, 2010, 2012, and 2014, making a total of 12 appearances, twice on board one for his country.
