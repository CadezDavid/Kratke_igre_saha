  HeMateMe: Oddly enough, Iverson started his career in Philly as the point guard. That seems unlikely, but he played point his first season. Talanted guy, he could play either spot. After season one, Philly realized they had a playmaker who would rather shoot than run plays, so they moved him to two guard.

I realize that Philly had bad luck drafting players, and AI was left to carry the burden, but I still think his style kills teams, in the long run.

This is a good argument for putting a hard cap in the NBA, as is the case in pro football. The luxury tax is useless, it doesn't stop teams from overspending to steal other teams best players, or to ensure that no one can take their own players.

If a hard cap was in place, maybe the Sixers could have gotten a low post player with good hands, a big guy who could score, and take some pressure off of the back court. Without a true cap, no team will give up a good big man, they will just spend into the luxury tax. Small market teams suffer, as a result. Whole baseball teams have been run with just the money that Alex Rodriguez makes. Baseball is even a bigger disgrace.

Feb-22-14  Jim Bartle: Why so disdainful about Gilligan's Island?

Auerbach loved Russell as a prospect, and knew the team ahead of him (Syracuse) wouldn't take him, as they had Maurice Stokes (though not for long, tragically). They took journeyman Sihugo Green. I really don't know what the other teams thought of Russell, except everyone knew he couldn't shoot.

He had an exciting team with Bob Cousy (whom he didn't want at first), but needed a more powerful center.

So Auerbach traded Hall of Fame center Easy Ed Macauley and future All-Star Cliff Hagan for Russell, who was the second pick. (Actually the third, as the Celts had taken Heinsohn with their territorial pick.)

The Hawks remained a very good team, winning a championship, but of course falling behind the Celts. One reason may be that they didn't have any black players until 1961, and that guy was ostracized and didn't play at all. Only later did they get decent black players like Beaty, Bridges, Wilkens and Caldwell.
