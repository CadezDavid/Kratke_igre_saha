  Once: I suspect that black's spidey sense was not working at the time. Lulled into a false sense of security perhaps. This is the position one move before the POTD:


click for larger view

White has just played the seemingly passive 13. Bc1. White's Nc7 doesn't seem to be a threat - it just loses the knight. The Ba6 and Ne4 are both safe. Black's king looks safely tucked away. The e8 square isn't attacked at all. Arguably, we're still in the opening.

So what could possibly go wrong with the "normal" developing move of 13...Re8? The position seems safe, quiet, developpy. BTW, "developpy" is a new word for those of us who get confused by the spelling of <manooooover>. There's an O or two and mebbe an E and a U and ... oh, I don't know. Spelling never was my strong suit.

Anyhoo, back to the chess. If look a little deeper we see that either white is happy to see us or there is a gun in his pocket. There is a potential discovered attack on the e8 square. This means that in just one move the e8 square could go from being not attacked at all to being twice attacked. And that spells danger, Will Robinson, danger.

I guess we've all been there and played a move like 13...Re8 without seeing the risks. The moral of the story seems to be ... think long and hard before standing in the cross-hairs of a discovered attack.
