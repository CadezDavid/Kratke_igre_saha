Mar-05-07  Aldo Bonatti: This is at present my only game edited in chess data bases. J must to confess that the idea of winning move 13).....,Nxc3! is not original since already played in Hajek-Buresh (see my comment of that game).The move 13.h5 instead of 13.g3 not prevent the Black's combination. The only moves for White are the previous 12.Nxe4! instead of 12.ooo?! or 12.g3!? and after this move and 12)......,Re8 or 12)....d5 13.Nxe4! with uncertain play or after 12)ooo?! Re8 13.Nxe4 ! played in Pablo-Gil Gonzalez (J'm to prepare the comment of this game). Are also played 13.d5? in Hurt-Baca and Craig-Iavanez Rico but 13).....Nxc3! is always a winnig move. The move 13.Nc4 played in Skotorenko-Reprinuev
perhaps prevent Nxc3 but it is insufficient for White in order ti equalize.The move 13.Re1 played in Holmes-Hebden,Carroll-Curnow and Maes-Winants prevent Nxc3 but not the Black's attach to White castle (the pawn a2 is weak and therefore the diagonal a2-g8). After 13)...Nxc3 whewer 14.bxc3 (acceptance of sacrifice) or 14.Qxe7 (refusal) are losing. For example 14.bxc3 Qxw2 15.Bxe2 Rxe2 16.Ne1 d5 17.Kb2 Be7; 14.Qxe7 Nxa2+!(not 14).....,Nxe7?15.bxc3 Nd5 16.c4 =)15.Kb1 and now whewer 15).....,Rxe7 or 15).....,Bxe7 give a decisive advantage to Black. The question is if the Black for try yo convert the material advantage in development advantage should be able to anticipate the change of Knight in e4 playing suddently instead of 11).....,ooo 11).....,Nxc3 (!? or ?! ?). For example 11)..... Nxc3 12.bxc3 (of course the sacrifice's refusal leave the Black with two pawns plus)OOO 13.Bg5 f6 14.Qxe7 Bxe7 15.Bf4 Rhe8 16.Be2 d5 17.Kd1 Ba3 18.Rb1 RE6 19.Bf1 Na5 20.Rb5 b6 21.Nb1 Bf8 22.Ne1 Rde8 etc (analyse with Fritz) and probably Black have only the compensation for the material.
